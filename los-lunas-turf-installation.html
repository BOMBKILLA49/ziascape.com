The type of artificial grass we use depends on the project & the customer requirements. For many applications in New Mexico we use a high-strength Polyethylene artificial turf. Commonly used where higher compressive strength is required. This is often the material of choice for high-quality artificial grass due to its balance of softness and durability.
Artificial grass, being synthetic, does not require a curing process in the same way that concrete or some natural materials do. However, there are a few processes and considerations post-installation that could be likened to "curing" in a broader sense.
Low Maintenance: No need for mowing, watering, or fertilizing. Consistent Appearance: Maintains a green, lush look year-round. Water Conservation: Saves water since it doesn't need irrigation. Durability: Resistant to weather changes, heavy use, and wear. Pet-Friendly: Many varieties are designed to withstand pet activity, with features like drainage for urine.
High-quality artificial grass can last between 15 to 25 years with proper installation and maintenance. The lifespan can vary based on the quality of the grass, the intensity of use, and environmental factors.
